The Core Problem

Why Timing Matters More
Than Trend Following

Trend strategies work — until they don't. The real risk isn't being wrong. The real cost is structural:

  • Entering a position after the move has already begun
  • Exiting after the reversal has already started
  • Giving back profits while waiting for confirmation

Cycle-based timing doesn't replace your analysis — it tells you when markets are most statistically likely to transition from consolidation into a new directional trend. That's the window where risk/reward is most favorable.

A Note on Cycle Analysis

Markets are influenced by many factors — supply, demand, policy, and geopolitical events. Cycle analysis does not predict those events directly. Instead, it identifies periods when markets are statistically more likely to transition from one phase to another. These windows often align with major shifts in sentiment, positioning, and volatility.

The Grain Timing Report is designed to highlight those windows before the move becomes obvious to the broader market.
